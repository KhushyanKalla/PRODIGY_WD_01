Statistics

1. **Global Population**: As of 2024, the world’s population is estimated to be around 8 billion people.
2. **Life Expectancy**: The global average life expectancy is approximately 73 years. This varies significantly between countries, with higher life expectancies in developed nations and lower ones in developing regions.
3. **Internet Usage**: Over 5.5 billion people have access to the internet, which is about 70% of the global population.
4. **Languages Spoken**: There are roughly 7,000 languages spoken around the world today, though many are endangered with only a few speakers remaining.
5. **Urbanization**: About 57% of the global population lives in urban areas, a number that has been steadily increasing over the past few decades.
6. **Literacy Rates**: The global literacy rate for adults is around 87%, with significant variations between different regions and countries.
7. **Average Daily Screen Time**: On average, people spend about 6 to 7 hours per day interacting with screens, including smartphones, computers, and televisions.
8. **Food Production**: Over 70% of the world’s arable land is used for food production, supporting a global food system that feeds billions of people daily.

Information

1. **Brain Complexity**: The human brain contains approximately 86 billion neurons, each forming thousands of connections, making it one of the most complex and powerful organs in the body.
2. **Genetic Similarity**: Humans share about 99.9% of their DNA with one another. This genetic similarity underscores the common ancestry and biological unity of our species.
3. **Unique Fingerprints**: No two individuals have identical fingerprints. This uniqueness makes them a reliable method for identification.
4. **Human Diversity**: There are over 2,000 ethnic groups around the world, each with its own unique culture, language, and traditions.

The Human Brain's Capacity: The human brain is capable of generating approximately 23 watts of power while awake, enough to power a dim light bulb.
